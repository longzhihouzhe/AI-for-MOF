Model Explanation

This predictive tool utilizes a multivariate linear regression model developed to quantify the influence of molecular features on the zeta potential of lanthanide metal-organic frameworks (Ln-MOFs). The model integrates four key structural descriptors to enable precise predictions:

Carboxyl Groups (C)

Each carboxyl group contributes to a more negative zeta potential, with a regression coefficient of -3.784.

Amino Groups (A)

Protonated amino groups increase positive surface charge, with a regression coefficient of +4.11.

Benzene Rings (B)

The influence of benzene rings on zeta potential is modeled by -19.709B + 2.887B², which reflects a complex relationship between aromatic conjugation and surface charge density.

ESP Difference (E)

Although ESP Difference is not directly in the equation, it can be an important factor in understanding the charge distribution of MOFs.

The model was trained on multiple ligands and demonstrated exceptional accuracy.
